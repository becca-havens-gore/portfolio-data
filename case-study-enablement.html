Executive Summary

As Rapid7's design organization grew, new designers, engineers, and PMs needed a consistent foundation for design thinking, problem framing, platform UX patterns, and system-compliant decision-making. Meanwhile, the increasing complexity of the platform increased the risk of misalignment, inconsistent onboarding, and siloed learning.

I led a multi-year initiative to enable and empower the next generation of designers through mentorship, structured learning programs, cross-functional design thinking workshops, design operations, and emerging AI-assisted education tools. Together with design leaders, PMs, engineers, and content partners, we built a sustainable culture of shared learning, creative confidence, and system fluency—helping new and experienced contributors deliver higher quality work with greater velocity and autonomy.

Context

Rapid7's design organization expanded significantly in size, responsibility, and scope, creating new challenges:

  • New designers entering a highly complex platform
  • Diverse skill levels across the organization
  • Acquired design teams merging with existing ones
  • Multi-product UX patterns to learn and follow
  • Increasing importance of platform-level thinking
  • Large amounts of tribal knowledge with no central home

At the same time, teams increasingly needed:

  • Faster onboarding for new team members
  • Shared mental models across disciplines
  • Modern design skills development
  • Confidence working with system constraints
  • Access to design and research best practices
  • Guidance using AI in design responsibly

The Problem

Without structured enablement:

  • Designers duplicated work across teams
  • New hires struggled to understand platform conventions
  • PMs and engineers lacked design language fluency
  • Junior designers lacked clear mentorship pathways
  • Workshops and cross-functional alignment were inconsistent
  • AI was underutilized or misused without design guidance

Result: Design quality and velocity were uneven, onboarding took too long, and teams missed opportunities to collaborate effectively or to scale good practices.

Research & Discovery

I synthesized insights from multiple sources to understand the enablement needs:

Research Methods

  • Conversations with junior and mid-level designers
  • Manager feedback and team assessments
  • PM and engineering onboarding pain points
  • Design maturity assessments
  • Team retrospectives
  • Learning & development interviews

Key Insights

1. Design onboarding lacked structure

New designers struggled to understand platform architecture and system constraints.

2. Mentorship was informal and uneven

Growth depended on who you happened to work with, not a structured program.

3. Cross-functional teams needed shared vocabulary

PMs and engineers wanted to understand design thinking frameworks.

4. Designers wanted career support

Especially around systems thinking, collaboration, and problem framing.

5. AI education was missing

Teams were experimenting without a clear framework for responsible use.

Strategy & Enablement Architecture

I built a multi-layered design enablement strategy encompassing four pillars:

⭐ Pillar 1: Mentorship & Designer Growth

1:1 Mentorship

I mentored designers across levels on:

  • System thinking and platform UX
  • Storytelling and case study development
  • Interaction design craft
  • Cross-functional collaboration
  • Career growth and role clarity

Designer Learning Plans

I co-created individualized growth plans including:

  • Skill maps and assessments
  • Measurable milestones
  • Portfolio guidance
  • Regular feedback loops

Peer Learning Rituals

  • Portfolio reviews
  • Critique circles
  • Skill showcases

⭐ Pillar 2: Design Education & Curriculum

I designed a structured internal design curriculum covering:

Foundations

  • UX process & product thinking
  • Problem framing
  • Information architecture
  • Interaction patterns
  • Accessibility
  • Research methods

Platform UX

  • Rapid7 platform philosophy
  • RDS usage and best practices
  • Object-centric design
  • Listing/Details/Dashboard patterns

System Architecture

  • Tokens, components, templates
  • How pattern governance works
  • When/how proposals are evaluated

This accelerated onboarding and expanded design maturity across teams.

⭐ Pillar 3: Cross-Functional Design Thinking Workshops

To increase collaboration and design literacy across organizations, I hosted and facilitated:

Workshop Topics

  • Problem Framing
  • Journey Mapping
  • Opportunity Identification
  • Cross-team triad working sessions
  • "Designing together" alignment workshops

Outcomes

  • PMs better understood UX's role
  • Engineers understood interaction models
  • Teams generated shared definitions of success
  • Workshops enabled faster product alignment

⭐ Pillar 4: AI-Powered Design Learning Tools

This is one of the most forward-looking contributions.

System-Aware AI Education Tools

I partnered with system designers and engineers to develop:

  • AI copilots trained on RDS
  • Tools that explain interaction patterns
  • Suggestion engines that recommend system components
  • AI summaries of UX docs for onboarding
  • Conversational learning assistants in Miro & Supernova

AI Capabilities

AI assists designers with questions like:

  • "Which pattern should I use for X?"
  • "Show me usage guidelines for this component."
  • "Explain the accessibility rules for modals."
  • "What is our filtering standard?"

Benefits

  • Dramatically reduces onboarding time
  • Increases RDS adoption
  • Provides consistent design guidance
  • Prevents system drift during design exploration
