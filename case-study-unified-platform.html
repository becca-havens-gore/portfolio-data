Executive Summary

Across Rapid7's product suite, fundamental interactions—tables, filters, batch actions, and data workflows—were implemented differently in every product. This fragmentation caused inconsistent user experiences, increased cognitive load, and significant engineering rework.

I led the UX strategy, system architecture, and design for the Unified Platform Experience, establishing consistent interaction patterns built on accessible, scalable, MUI-based components. Working with engineering, UX leadership, and product teams across the portfolio, we delivered a cohesive experience layer that improved usability, reduced design and engineering drift, and increased customer trust in the platform.

Context

Rapid7's platform includes multiple products, each with unique implementations of core interactions. These workflows represent 70–85% of high-frequency user actions across Rapid7 products, making cohesion critical.

Each team designed these independently, resulting in:

  • Fractured experiences across products
  • Users re-learning interactions between products
  • Inconsistent accessibility support
  • High engineering overhead
  • Difficulty unifying data workflows for future platform integration

The Problem

Core Issues

  • Tables behaved differently everywhere (hover, click, select, expand, sort)
  • Filtering models varied (left rail, horizontal, query bar, chip-based)
  • Batch actions were inconsistent (placement, visibility, rules)
  • Accessibility issues (keyboard traps, missing ARIA, unpredictable focus)
  • Engineers rebuilt tables in different ways, creating long-term maintenance problems

User Impact

  • Analysts were slower to triage issues
  • Managers couldn't easily compare data across products
  • New users needed to relearn patterns repeatedly
  • Errors increased due to interaction unpredictability

Business Impact

  • High cost to build and maintain duplicate tables
  • Reduced confidence in platform maturity
  • Increased training and onboarding time

Research & Synthesis

I synthesized findings from multiple research streams to understand the full scope of the problem:

Research Methods

  • Field studies with security analysts
  • Customer support ticket analysis
  • Heuristic evaluation across products
  • Usability testing across product suite
  • Cross-product pattern inventory

Key Research Insights

1. Inconsistency increases cognitive load

Analysts struggled moving between products due to pattern drift. Each product required re-learning fundamental interactions.

2. Predictability is critical

Users needed consistent sorting, filtering, and selection to reduce error risk, especially during high-pressure incident response.

3. Visibility matters

Batch actions hidden behind menus were frequently missed, leading to inefficient workflows and repetitive manual actions.

4. Accessibility gaps blocked workflows

Keyboard traps, missing focus indicators, and inconsistent selection patterns caused significant blockers for keyboard-dependent users.

5. Filtering is a power feature

Users wanted filtering to act the same across products with predictable chip behaviors and query logic.

Synthesis Themes

  • Normalize behaviors, not just visuals
  • Reduce re-learning friction
  • Improve clarity and scannability
  • Enable scale through systemization

Strategy & Architecture

I architected UPX around three foundational pillars:

Pillar 1: Interaction Frameworks

DataGrid Interactions:

  • Hover states
  • Row and cell focus behavior
  • Selection patterns
  • Expand/collapse logic
  • Right-click behavior
  • Inline editing
  • Keyboard navigation

Filtering:

  • Horizontal filters
  • Query-chip integration
  • Clear-all behavior
  • Time-range filtering models

Batch Actions:

  • Appears only when items are selected
  • Contextual toolbar
  • Apply/undo flows
  • Accessible button grouping

Pillar 2: RDS Component Backing

Working closely with RDS engineering, we ensured UPX patterns were built on:

  • MUI DataGrid foundation
  • Shared toolbar and button patterns
  • Figma ↔ code parity
  • Token-driven theming

This guaranteed long-term maintainability and consistency across the platform.

Pillar 3: Platform Templates

To enforce cross-product consistency:

  • Listing template
  • Details template
  • Dashboard template
  • Filtering ribbon component
  • Batch action toolbar component

Every product team now used the same templates, accelerating onboarding and reducing design & engineering lift.

Design Principles

  • Predictability — Same input → same output, regardless of product
  • Scannability — Optimized spacing, typography, and motion
  • Accessibility First — Keyboard-operable, screen-reader compatible, high contrast
  • Efficiency — Reduce clicks, ambiguity, and unnecessary interactions
  • Scalability — Patterns should support future platform features and AI
